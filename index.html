Process · 005
Fig. 01 — Harvest, pre-dawn
Étape 01
Harvest— pre-dawn · 4°C
The rose field at Isparta is walked between 03:00 and 05:00, when the petals still carry night-water. A single passage, never twice.
Fig. 02 — Distillation vessel
Étape 02
Distillation— copper still, 14h
Low-and-slow in hand-hammered copper. We pull only the heart cut. Heads and tails are returned to the field as compost.
Fig. 03 — Cold press, -4°C
Étape 03
Cold press— argon atmosphere
Oils are expressed below freezing in an oxygen-free chamber. Yield drops by two thirds; molecular integrity holds.
Fig. 04 — Blending room
Étape 04
Composition— Atelier N° IX
Seventeen extracts are read like a symphony. The perfumer-formulator tests each lot for forty days before approval.
Fig. 05 — Filling, argon-sealed
Étape 05
Fill & seal— zero O₂
Each bottle is filled under argon, stoppered, and numbered by hand. The pipette is ground glass — never plastic.
Fig. 06 — Final assay
Étape 06
Assay— 48-hour quarantine
Every batch rests for two days before release. Independent HPLC confirms active concentrations within 0.5%.
Eight weeks.
One harvest.
A thousand bottles.

Origin · 010

A letter from
the founder.

I grew up between a rose field and a perfume bench. My grandmother taught me that a bottle is a promise — that whatever you put inside must be worth the glass around it.

For most of my career I watched that promise be broken, quietly, at every stage of the industry. Zoho is my answer. Not a brand. A correction.

Eight weeks, eighteen ingredients, one bottle at a time. If that sounds slow, good — it is. It always was.

— Noëlle Aubert
